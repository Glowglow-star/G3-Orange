Task 1 · Spatial Analysis
Where do stops happen?
Stop frequency by SFPD district, revealing which neighborhoods see the highest policing activity. Southern and Ingleside districts lead significantly.
Interpretation

The district distribution suggests that stop activity is concentrated rather than evenly spread across the city. Southern and Ingleside stand out as the strongest clusters, while several other districts show noticeably lower stop volume.

This pattern may reflect differences in roadway design, traffic intensity, patrol strategy, or enforcement priorities. Because these are raw stop counts, they should be interpreted as a measure of policing concentration rather than a direct measure of neighborhood danger.

Key Takeaway

A relatively small number of districts account for a large share of observed stops. This makes geography one of the most important filters for understanding where enforcement is experienced most intensely across San Francisco.
